Industry Glossary

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing)

The manufacturer produces goods based on the buyer's specifications and brand. The factory handles production; the client owns the brand and marketing.

ODM (Original Design Manufacturing)

The manufacturer designs and produces products that the buyer then brands as their own. The factory contributes R&D and design expertise.

SAP (Super Absorbent Polymer)

A granular material that can absorb and retain extremely large amounts of liquid relative to its mass. Core component in sanitary pads and adult diapers.

ISO 13485

International standard for quality management systems in medical device manufacturing. Required for medical-grade adult diapers sold in the EU.

GSM (Grams per Square Meter)

The weight of material per square meter. Used to specify the thickness and absorbency of non-woven fabric layers in hygiene products.

AQL (Acceptable Quality Level)

A statistical sampling method used in quality control. AQL 2.5 means no more than 2.5% of the batch may have defects.

CE Marking

Certification indicating conformity with EU health, safety, and environmental standards. Required for hygiene products sold in European markets.

MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)

The smallest quantity a manufacturer will produce per order. Our MOQ is 50,000 pcs per SKU for sanitary pads and 30,000 pcs for adult diapers.

Non-Woven Fabric

Fabric-like material made from fibers bonded together. Used as the top sheet (surface layer) in sanitary pads and adult diapers for softness and fluid transfer.

Fluff Pulp

Wood pulp processed into a fluffy, absorbent material. Used alongside SAP in the absorbent core to wick and distribute liquid.

FOB (Free On Board)

Shipping term meaning the seller delivers goods to the port and loads them on the vessel. The buyer assumes responsibility and cost from that point.

T/T Payment

Telegraphic Transfer —a common international payment method. Our standard terms: 30% deposit upfront, 70% balance before shipment.
